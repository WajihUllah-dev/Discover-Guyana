Mount Roraima – The Lost World

Towering at 9,200 feet, Mount Roraima is one of the oldest geological formations on Earth, straddling the borders of Guyana, Venezuela, and Brazil. Often shrouded in mist, this mysterious tabletop mountain inspired Sir Arthur Conan Doyle’s novel The Lost World.

Why Visit Mount Roraima?

  • Unique Landscapes: Otherworldly rock formations, deep crevices, and quartz-filled valleys.
  • Biodiversity Hotspot: Home to species found nowhere else, including the Roraima Bush Toad.
  • Spectacular Views: See three countries from the summit.

Activities at Mount Roraima

  • Trekking Expedition: Embark on a 6-day hike through rainforests and rocky plateaus.
  • Exploring Hidden Valleys: Discover surreal landscapes like The Valley of Crystals.
  • Camping Above the Clouds: Spend nights in natural caves and stargaze from the summit.

How to Get There?

The journey begins with a flight from Georgetown to Lethem, followed by a guided trek starting in Venezuela.
